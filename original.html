Greetings CYM Families & Friends

First I want to express my deepest thanks and gratitude to my friends Janice, Thomas, and their son Samuel for coming to our Elementary class last Sunday and teaching us all about Chinese New Year, along with gifts and mementos for every student!  The children really enjoyed the experience and we are grateful for your generous sharing of this beautiful Chinese celebration.  What a gift!


This is a time of year when newness can be felt stirring beneath the surface.  Many cultures and religions celebrate festivals this time of year.  I know the Gregorian calendar we live by invited goal setting a month ago, but this is the time of year that I feel that energy inside of me.  I wonder if you feel it too?  And if so, what is stirring inside of you?  What can be hoped for and dreamed up now, as we start to feel that quiver of the seed?  There is important work to be done in the dark.  A garden that provides for the whole community in summer is diverse, works together, and supports its siblings.  Join us in community this Sunday to stir up whatever kind of energy you might be needing.

THIS SUNDAY: Imbolc Ritual February 2 from 1PM - 2:30 PM

The eclectic pagan group of UUSE is hosting an Imbolc ritual open to the public of all ages and practices. Please join us! This ritual will be inspired by Scottish tradition and folklore.  There is a small, natural spring on the property which is where the ritual will begin. (This portion will also be livestreamed to an indoor location for folks who may need to or prefer to stay indoors.)   


The water will not be flowing on Imbolc, and we will invite the water to flow again. We will honor the long held Scottish belief that wells are a place of healing and fertility, inviting the water to return through vernal goddess Bride (Brigid) as well as encouraging the Cailleach (Scottish winter crone of the woods) to lay down for winter's end.  Outdoor participants will then proceed indoors for the second portion of the ritual which will include a time for reflection and music, followed by a casual potluck. 


Dress however you are comfortable; symbolic colors include white, green, and fiery hues, and tartan or plaid is another option. Foods associated with the festival can include breads, cheeses, blackberries, cold hardy greens, but anything goes. Dress warm if you plan to attend the first portion outdoors, and a camping chair is welcome. (Outdoor portion will be relatively short.)  Families of children and youth are welcome to attend!  It is open and advertised to the public, you may find the Facebook Event here: https://www.facebook.com/share/19r9RHNLXq/
